Investing Tiger: An investing playground for fun and education


Albert Einstein once said, Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it

We did not understand him at first, like all of his other theories! Duh! So, we went ahead and tried it ourselves.

We asked ourselves a question - how much would our money grow if we invest Rs. 30k every month for 10 years at an expected returns of 10% per annum? We would have invested 36 Lakhs and we would get ~Rs. 61 lakh at the end! Impressive!

Then we asked - how much would our money grow in the same scenario but if we kept increasing the SIP amount by 10% every year. We would have invested ~57 lakhs and we would get ~90 lakh! Not too bad!

Now we became curious (and greedy)! We wanted to see how our money would grow for 20 years or, let's say, 30 years and what if our returns were more than just 10%, let's say, 15% or 25% (that's Warren Buffet level returns btw!)

Very tiresome to iterate again and again, right? Why don't you put these numbers in the form below and see the results in a nice graph for yourself?

Oh and we saw the numbers and, to quote Mr. Ranvijay from Roadies, We were stunned! because now we understand what Einstein meant!

Go ahead. Try for yourself.
